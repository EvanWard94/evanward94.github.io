A. User Experience Requirements

1. Related User Stories

  Relevant to our problem as it relates to face-to-face instruction, there are several users that can be identified. While there are two main user groups, instructors and students, they can be broken down further into sub groups for the purpose of creating user stories. The groups selected are as follows: instructor users, student users with hearing impairments, student users whose first language is not English, and student users who speak primarily English and do not have hearing impairments.

User Stories: Instructor Users
  1. As an instructor, I want to upload a video with closed captioning so all students can visually view what I am saying as it corresponds with the video.
  2. As an instructor, I want to upload a video with transcripts so all students can visually see the full video in text form.
  3. As an instructor, I hope to add closed captioning to my video with ease so that I do not have to spend extra time adding closed captioning to the videos.
  4. As an instructor, I hope to add transcripts to my video with ease so that I do not have to spend extra time transcribing them myself.
  5. As an instructor, I hope to add closed captioning to my video without any additional cost so that I will not have to outsource the video and spend extra money.
  6. As an instructor, I hope to add transcripts to my video without any additional cost so that I will not have to outsource the video and spend extra money.
  7. As an instructor, I want to add closed captioning with at least 99% accuracy so that students with hearing impairments or English not as their first language are able to understand what I am saying.
  8. As an instructor, I want to add transcripts with at least 99% accuracy so that students with hearing impairments or English not as their first language are able to understand what I am saying.
  9. As an instructor, my closed captioning should be flagged for potential inaccuracies so that the video’s closed captioning is at least 99% accurate.
  10. As an instructor, the transcripts should be flagged for any potential inaccuracies so that the video’s transcripts are at least 99% accurate.
User Stories: Student Users Whose First Language is Not English
  1. As a student whose first language is not English, I want closed captions on my instructors videos so that I can read some text while the instructor is speaking and follow along more easily.
  2. As a student whose first language is not English, I want a transcript available to view so that I can just read the instruction rather than slowly follow along with a video and pause when I’m not sure what the instructor is saying.
  3. As a student whose first language is not English, I want a transcript of the words my instructor says in a video available so that if there is a word or sentence I may not understand I can copy and paste it to a translation app rather than try to find someone else to translate it for me.
  4. As a student whose first language is not English, I want a transcript of the video my professor posted with the ability to translate it to my native language so that I can read the instruction in a language familiar to me and better understand it.
User Stories: Student Users Who Have Hearing Impairment
  1. As a student with a hearing impairment, I want to view all audio and video content uploaded to ELC with the option to turn closed-captioning on.
  2. As a student with a hearing impairment, I want to view all audio and video content uploaded to ELC with the option to download a corresponding transcript.
  3. As a student with a hearing impairment, I hope that the closed-captioning on the audio and video content I am accessing on ELC has at least 99% accuracy so that I can understand the content being viewed with ease.
  4. As a student with a hearing impairment, I hope that the transcripts corresponding to any audio and video content have at least 99% accuracy so that I can understand the content being viewed with ease.
  5. As a student with a hearing impairment, I want all closed-captioning on audio and video content to be accessed directly in ELC, so that I do not have to visit another platform to access this option.
  6. As a student with a hearing impairment, I want all transcripts corresponding to audio and video content to be accessed directly in ELC, so that I do not have to visit another platform to access this option.
User Stories: Student Users Who Speak Primarily English and Do Not Have Hearing Impairments
  1. As an English-speaking student without a hearing impairment, I want the option to view subtitles, so that I have no difficulty understanding the professor’s speech.
  2. As an English-speaking student without a hearing impairment, I want to view a transcript of all videos, so that I can repurpose them into my own notes.
  3. As an English-speaking student without a hearing impairment, I want all captions and transcripts to be accurate, so that their purpose is not defeated through unintelligible or missing text.
  4. As an English-speaking student without a hearing impairment, I want to download a transcript of uploaded videos, so that I can have access to their contents while I am offline or while ELC is undergoing maintenance.
  5. As an English-speaking student without a hearing impairment, I want all captions and transcripts to be accessible through ELC, so that I do not have to unnecessarily access another service.
